Algorithmic Hiring Bias

During my sophomore year of college, I decided to apply for summer internships. After I submitted many applications to similar companies, many required that I complete a "game" at the screening stage of the hiring process to determine whether I got an interview. I quickly became curious how a game could determine whether I deserve an interview and discovered that companies use algorithmic hiring to compare applicants' performances on these games to current employees to screen them. This instantly worried me as someone who is a minority in the careers I am pursuing. If companies are comparing me to their past employees, a group of people that does not contain many like myself, how will the companies judge me fairly with these "games"?
I decided to research algorithmic hiring bias with a focus on gender and created a presentation about this process to explain why companies use them, the bias within them, the research priorities and engineering innovation processes, and next steps.

Slide Presentation

I created this slide deck to assist my scripted presentation for adults that are familiar with algorithmic hiring but do not know much about bias in this process.
I had given many speeches before giving this presentation, so the greatest area of focus was on the slide deck. I focused on visuals to ensure the audience focuses on the words the speaker says. Instead of just using bullet points to follow my speaking points, I utilized colors, diagrams, and pictures to make the presentation more engaging. I also utilized black slides to ensure listeners would not be distracted by the screen when necessary.
This presentation connects with my passion for equity and my desire to explore and understand fields related to technology. Exploring algorithmic hiring bias allows me to explore equity in hiring processes and how math and machine learning can contribute and fight discrimination in algorithmic hiring. This artifact also taught me how to effectively educate others on this topic. This artifact allowed me to improve my teaching skills and connect technology and equity communication, which is my main goal as a science communicator.
